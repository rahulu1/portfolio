The Portfolio of Rahul Unniyampath

Projects

  • Metroid (Unity Remaster): My early development on the project focused on recreating the door and camera systems authentically. Specific challenges at this stage included finding a way to mimic the unique resource management system of the original game, wherein out-of-view enemies became inactive, and all rooms besides the current one ceased to run. In the more advanced stages of the project, my contributions were primarily towards the design of the custom mechanic and its ecosystem of related components. I was also the lead techincal artist and implemented all the animations, related state machines, and necessary custom sprites.
    Technologies used in the development of this project include C# and Unity for the primary game mechanic recreation and level design. The custom art assets were designed entirely on Pixilart, an online pixel art editing site. My artwork for this project can be found on my profile there.
  • Tiltshift Knockout!: This game was the result of a 2-week rapid prototyping endeavor. My goal with this game was to quickly develop an incredibly powerful modular driving system, and then use that to create a game with arcadey, physics-based combat. The game sees two players compete to be the last one standing by dashing and crashing into each other in an attempt to knock their opponent off the arena. There are two available gamemodes at the moment, and one vehicle class to choose from, with another in development.
